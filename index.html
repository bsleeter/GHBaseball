A Historic Season

Under head coach Ben Sleeter, the 2025 Tides put together one of the best seasons in program history. Gig Harbor opened the year by knocking off defending state champion West Seattle and seventh-ranked Rogers, surging to the No. 1 spot in the MaxPreps Class 3A state rankings.

The Tides dominated the regular season with a 15-1 conference record, outscoring opponents by wide margins behind elite pitching and a defense that committed just seven errors all season. In the district tournament, the Tides shut out Capital 3-0 and edged Kelso 1-0 to earn the state berth.

At the Class 3A state tournament, Gig Harbor beat West Seattle 3-2 and Inglemoor 6-2 to reach the Final Four before falling to Kennewick 12-5 in the semifinals. The Tides finished 27-3 with a legacy of excellence that sets the standard heading into 2026.

About Tides Baseball

The Gig Harbor High School Tides Baseball program is committed to developing student-athletes both on and off the field. Our program competes in the South Puget Sound League (3A) and fields Varsity, JV, and C Team squads each spring.

Coming off a historic 2025 campaign that saw the Tides go 27-3, earn the No. 1 state ranking, and reach the Class 3A Final Four, the program is poised to build on that success in 2026 under head coach Ben Sleeter.

We emphasize fundamentals, teamwork, sportsmanship, and academic achievement. Home games are played at Sehmel Homestead Park. The 2026 season begins with tryouts on March 2nd.
